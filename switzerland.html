What my X and I saw in Switzerland


Mountains and lash green landscapes

Switzerland is where serenity meets grandeur—an alpine dream sculpted by nature’s hand. I like the natural beauty of the mountains and landscapes: the morning mist, emerald valleys cradling mirror-like lakes, and winding trails that disappear into the clouds.

The vibrant but calm Lucerne. The city is in middle of Swiss Alps and surrounded by beautiful Lucerne lake. If you visit the place once, you will surely comeback again and again.


A day starts on the snowy Alps. An old monument's ruins watch it.


I wait for the moment when the sun kisses snow-capped summits, casting golden hues across untouched terrain. The silence here is profound, broken only by the whisper of wind through pine forests or the distant chime of cowbells. Through my lens, Switzerland becomes more than a destination—it’s a feeling of awe, solitude, and connection to something vast and eternal. These landscapes aren’t just photographed; they’re experienced, one breathless frame at a time.

A Swiss village near the city Interlaken. The tranquillity of the village will make you forget the hussles of a city.


The mountians on the other side of Lucerne lake. You need to be lucky to find the sunny light on an average day in Lucerne.


I loved Lake Brienz so much that I spent most of my time around it when I was in Interlaken. The tuquise blue colour of the water and the serene environment in the midst of Swis Alps were so enchanting that I forgot everything else.
