What my X and I found in London


Timeless in Central London

Central London pulses with a rhythm that’s both timeless and electric—a city where history and innovation collide in every frame. London’s skyline offers a dynamic canvas for cityscape photography. I chase moments when the golden hour softens the edges of Victorian architecture, and when rain-slicked pavements reflect neon lights like brushstrokes on a living painting.

London Eye on a beautiful sunny morning, as seen from the Westminster Bridge. London remains cloudy on many days in a year, so a sunny day means a lot to London and the people there.


The other side of river Thames seen from the east side. The St. Paul's Cathedral is on the left and on the extreme right you can see the famous "Walkie-Talkie" building.


Whether it’s the quiet majesty of St. Paul’s Cathedral or the kinetic energy of Piccadilly Circus, each image tells a story of contrast—old and new, stillness and motion. My lens seeks not just buildings, but the heartbeat of the city: the way light dances across the Thames, the symmetry of shadows in hidden alleyways, and the fleeting expressions of Londoners caught in the rush. This is London, not just seen—but felt.

A Central London street on a sunny Sunday morning. The golden light on the street creates the lively and energetic mood on the people.


The iconic London red bus that has become a symbol of the city. This red bus is plying behind the Parliament house at the Central London.
